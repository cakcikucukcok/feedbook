This book offers students an invaluable look into what cultural anthropologists do when they are in the field. With fascinating insights from their life and work, the authors describe diverse forms of fieldwork, common problems encountered in the field, and the many ways in which scholars interpret the data they collect. Through teaching students to understand how and why anthropologists ask questions and the techniques they use to answer those questions, In the Field makes a powerful case for the value of the anthropological approach to knowledge.
